1. election_analysis.png - Polling vs Prediction Markets Time Series Analysis
This figure presents a comprehensive comparison between traditional polling data and prediction market prices for the 2024 US Presidential election from August 2024 to October 2024. Key observations:

a) Polling Trends (Solid Lines):
- Harris (Blue Solid): Shows remarkably stable polling numbers, gradually increasing from 47% to 48.1% over the period
- Trump (Red Solid): Similarly stable, moving from 46% to 46.7%
- The polling gap between candidates remains relatively constant at ~1.4 percentage points

b) Prediction Markets (Dashed Lines):
- Harris (Blue Dashed): Shows significantly more volatility than polling
  * Started at ~42% in early August
  * Peaked at ~53% in mid-August
  * Sharp decline in October to ~33%
- Trump (Red Dashed): Mirror image of Harris's trajectory
  * Started at ~55%
  * Dipped to ~45% in mid-August
  * Strong surge in October reaching ~66%

c) Key Insights:
- Prediction markets show substantially more volatility than polling data
- Major divergence between polls and markets starting in October
- Markets predict a stronger position for Trump than polls suggest
- Polling data shows remarkable stability while markets react more dramatically to events
- The spread between candidates is much wider in prediction markets than in polls

This visualization effectively highlights the fundamental differences between these two forecasting methods:
1. Polls capture current voter preferences with high stability
2. Prediction markets appear to be more forward-looking and reactive to events
3. Markets show stronger confidence in Trump's position despite closer polling numbers

The stark contrast between stable polling and volatile market predictions raises important questions about which method might be more predictive of the final election outcome.
